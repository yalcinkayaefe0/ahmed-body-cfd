\section{Introduction}

The Ahmed body~\cite{ahmed1984} is a simplified bluff-body geometry that has become the standard benchmark for automotive aerodynamics CFD validation. Its geometry is fully parametric and its flow field—particularly the slant-angle-dependent wake structure—is extensively documented in the open literature.

This study uses the \textbf{25° slant angle} configuration validated against Lienhart \& Becker~\cite{lienhart2003} ($U_\infty = \SI{40}{m/s}$, $Re \approx 2.77\times10^6$, $C_d = 0.299 \pm 0.005$).

\subsection{Objectives}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Perform a three-level mesh independence study using the GCI methodology~\cite{celik2008}.
  \item Validate the fine-mesh $C_d$ against the experimental reference.
  \item Compare computed wake velocity profiles with Lienhart \& Becker (2003) at four downstream stations.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Mesh Independence Study}

\subsection{GCI Methodology}

For three meshes with cell counts $N_1 > N_2 > N_3$ and quantities $f_1, f_2, f_3$:
\begin{align}
  r_{21} &= \left(\tfrac{N_1}{N_2}\right)^{1/3}, \quad
  p = \frac{\left|\ln|\varepsilon_{32}/\varepsilon_{21}|\right|}{\ln r_{21}}, \\
  f_\text{ext} &= f_1 + \frac{f_1-f_2}{r_{21}^p-1}, \quad
  \text{GCI}_\text{fine} = \frac{1.25\,|e_{21,a}|}{r_{21}^p-1}
\end{align}

\section{Discussion}

\subsection{Drag Coefficient Underprediction}
The 9.7\% $C_d$ underprediction is consistent with the literature for $k$-$\omega$ SST with wall functions on bluff bodies. Key causes:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Wall function buffer region:} $y^+\approx15$–$30$ sits between the viscous sublayer and log-law validity, introducing near-wall velocity gradient errors.
  \item \textbf{RANS separation modelling:} $k$-$\omega$ SST underpredicts the slant recirculation zone, reducing base pressure drag.
  \item \textbf{Coarse mesh result:} The coarse mesh happened to give $C_d=0.299$, which matches the experiment closely. This is likely a coincidence — the numerical diffusion from the coarser cells probably masks the underprediction that shows up more clearly on finer meshes.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Lift Coefficient Discrepancy}
\label{sec:cl_disc}
The computed $C_l = -0.323$ deviates substantially from the Lienhart (2003) experimental
value of $-0.082\pm0.008$. Three factors are primarily responsible:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Support strut absence:} The experimental model is mounted on four support
  struts whose upwash partially unloads the underbody, reducing measured downforce.
  The CFD half-model omits the struts entirely, leaving the underbody flow unobstructed
  and generating significantly higher suction beneath the body.
  \item \textbf{Symmetry plane constraint:} The symmetry condition at $z=0$ enforces a
  perfectly symmetric flow field and suppresses the C-pillar vortex interaction present
  in the full geometry. This alters the integrated pressure on the slant and consequently
  the net lift.
  \item \textbf{RANS slant pressure sensitivity:} $k$-$\omega$ SST underpredicts the slant
  recirculation zone at 25°, producing a more attached pressure recovery over the rear
  ramp and a lower static pressure on the top surface — increasing downforce relative to
  experiment. Lift is a top-minus-bottom pressure integral and therefore accumulates these
  near-wall errors more strongly than drag.
\end{itemize}
A meaningful $C_l$ validation would require a full-model simulation with support struts
modelled explicitly and a low-$Re$ wall treatment ($y^+<1$) on the slant surface.

\subsection{Mesh Independence}
Observed order $p=1.14$ (vs.\ theoretical $\approx2$) is attributed to inconsistent $y^+$ across mesh levels from aspect-ratio BL sizing. A fixed first-layer thickness would yield consistent $y^+$ and higher $p$.

\subsection{Wake Profiles}
The wake trend matches the experimental profile — velocity deficit near the ground, recovering towards freestream above car height — but the deficit is overpredicted compared to Lienhart. The mean MAE across the four stations is around $0.30\,U/U_\infty$. Better agreement would probably need a finer near-wall mesh with $y^+<1$, or a more advanced simulation approach like LES.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Conclusions}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Fine mesh GCI = 5.3\% — near but not fully mesh-independent.
  \item $C_d = 0.270$ underpredicts Lienhart by 9.7\%, consistent with known $k$-$\omega$ SST + wall function limitations for separated bluff-body flow.
  \item Wake profiles qualitatively correct; quantitative improvement requires low-$Re$ wall treatment ($y^+<1$) and consistent $y^+$ across mesh levels.
\end{itemize}
